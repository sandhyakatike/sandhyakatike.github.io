Publications & Service

Published Paper

Title: Developing a Enterprise Application Tool to Discover Midwest Job Trends

Authors: Chandra Bathula (Saint Louis University), Maria Weber (Saint Louis University)

Presented at: CCSC - Central Plains 2025, Drake University, Iowa

Significant growth in Computer and Mathematical Occupations is projected...

Significant growth in Computer and Mathematical Occupations is projected over the next decade, outpacing other fields with higher median pay and demand for specialized skills. According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics (BLS), from 2023 to 2033, the U.S. anticipates an 11% growth in Computer Occupations and 7% in Mathematical Occupations, generating over 393,000 jobs annually [5][6][3]. This study analyzes national trends in these fields, focusing on wages, growth rates, and regional dynamics in the Central Plains (Iowa, Kansas, Missouri, Nebraska). Nationally, median salaries range from $101,000 to $104,000, with regional variations reflecting local economic factors [5][3]. Missouri leads regional growth, driven by demand for Data Scientists and Software Developers, while Kansas excels in Actuaries, and Iowa and Nebraska show steady increases in Information Security Analysts. This research highlights the importance of targeted skill development to meet evolving technological needs, using descriptive statistics and visualizations to inform educators, students, and policymakers.
